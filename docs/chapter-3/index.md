# Chapter 3: Digital Twins & Physics Simulation

This chapter explores digital twins and physics simulation techniques for humanoid robotics, covering how simulation environments enable safe development and testing of Physical AI systems before deployment to physical robots.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

- Understand the concept and importance of digital twins in robotics
- Explain physics simulation principles and their application to humanoid robots
- Implement simulation environments using Gazebo and Unity
- Connect simulation to real-world robotics through sim-to-real transfer
- Design effective simulation scenarios for humanoid robot development
- Evaluate simulation fidelity and its impact on real-world performance

## Prerequisites

To get the most out of this chapter, you should have:

- Understanding of ROS 2 concepts from Chapter 2
- Basic knowledge of physics and mechanics
- Familiarity with robotics kinematics and dynamics
- Understanding of the layered architecture from Chapter 1
